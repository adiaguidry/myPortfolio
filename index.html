GOALS PAY OFF

My final project was inspired by my six year old son, Noah. I wanted to teach him how to set goals for himself and I also wanted to teach him the value of a dollar. I thought it would be awesome to combined the two. After learning a bit of AngularJS I could see how two way data binding would be great for this project. As goals were being established by Noah, I would see those goals in the Admin interface, and they are listed for him to see on his interface. As he completes his goal he hits a button and I am notified. I decide how much he is paid for a job well done. I pay him through virtual money. That money is allocated to accounts, that are set up in admin, however he get to drag and drop to the account of his choice.

Admin Interface

Set up to 6 accounts with a title, goal amount, current amount. ex Pizza Night, $10.00, $0. Meaning they want a pizza night, they need $15.00 to hit that goal, and they have $0 for it as of now.
The other side tracks the kids goals. When goals are made they show up there. When they are completed you select an amount to pay them. The total increase until you hit payday!

Goal Interface

This is where the kids make their goals. As they input the goal it is bond to the side panel and shows instantly. It is also connect to AngularFire database. Each goal is put into the database by the child, and the parent handles the deletion of the goals upon completion.

Bank Interface

And here is where the GOALS PAY OFF! This is the bank which holds all the accounts that are set up by parent and child. Each time the child is paid they see the money appear. They decided which account to move their money into, and they are able to see their money grow as they get closer to their goals!
